==========================================
CODE OF CONDUCT & ETHICS
Version: 4.0 | Effective Date: January 1, 2024
==========================================

SECTION 1: CORE VALUES
----------------------------------------

1.1 OUR COMMITMENT
- Integrity: Do the right thing, always
- Respect: Treat everyone with dignity
- Excellence: Deliver quality in everything
- Innovation: Embrace new ideas
- Responsibility: Accountable for actions

1.2 SCOPE OF APPLICATION
- All employees: Full-time, part-time, contract
- Board of Directors
- Consultants and advisors
- Interns and trainees
- Business partners and vendors

SECTION 2: WORKPLACE CONDUCT
----------------------------------------

2.1 PROFESSIONAL BEHAVIOR
- Punctuality: Respect work hours
- Communication: Courteous and professional
- Collaboration: Support team objectives
- Dress code: Business casual or as per function
- Substance abuse: Zero tolerance

2.2 DISCRIMINATION & HARASSMENT
- Equal opportunity employer
- Prohibited: Race, gender, religion, age, disability
- Sexual harassment: Strictly prohibited
- Reporting: Multiple channels available
- Consequences: Termination + legal action

2.3 BULLYING PREVENTION
- Definition: Repeated, unreasonable behavior
- Examples: Threats, humiliation, exclusion
- Reporting: Anonymous hotline
- Investigation: Within 7 days
- Protection: No retaliation policy

SECTION 3: CONFLICT OF INTEREST
----------------------------------------

3.1 DISCLOSURE REQUIREMENTS
- Outside employment: Prior approval required
- Family members: Disclosure of reporting relationships
- Business interests: In competing companies prohibited
- Investments: >10% stake in competitor requires disclosure
- Board positions: Approval from CEO required

3.2 GIFTS & ENTERTAINMENT
- Accepting gifts: Max value ₹2,500 per instance
- Giving gifts: Within business norms
- Entertainment: Infrequent and reasonable
- Government officials: No gifts permitted
- Recording: All gifts tracked in system

3.3 FINANCIAL INTERESTS
- Insider trading: Strictly prohibited
- Stock transactions: Blackout periods enforced
- Confidential information: Cannot trade based on
- Material information: Only public knowledge
- Penalties: Legal action + termination

SECTION 4: CONFIDENTIAL INFORMATION
----------------------------------------

4.1 TYPES OF CONFIDENTIAL INFORMATION
- Trade secrets: Manufacturing processes
- Financial data: Budgets, forecasts, results
- Customer data: Contracts, pricing, PII
- Strategic plans: Mergers, acquisitions
- Intellectual property: Patents, designs

4.2 PROTECTION MEASURES
- Need-to-know: Limited access
- Non-disclosure: Signed agreements
- Document marking: Confidential stamps
- Digital security: Encryption required
- Exit interviews: Reminder of obligations

SECTION 5: INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY
----------------------------------------

5.1 COMPANY IP
- Ownership: All work product belongs to company
- Inventions: Assigned to company
- Patents: Filed in company name
- Copyright: Company owns all materials
- Third-party IP: Respect and license

5.2 OPEN SOURCE SOFTWARE
- Approval: Legal review required
- License compliance: GPL, MIT, etc.
- Attribution: Required as per license
- Contribution: Clearance from management
- Security: Vulnerability assessment

SECTION 6: DATA PRIVACY
----------------------------------------

6.1 PERSONAL DATA
- Collection: Only business-necessary
- Consent: Obtained where required
- Storage: Secure systems only
- Access: Need-to-know basis
- Deletion: Upon request (where applicable)

6.2 CUSTOMER DATA
- Use: Only for authorized purposes
- Sharing: Written consent required
- Retention: As per contract terms
- Breach: Report within 24 hours
- Compliance: GDPR, CCPA, IT Act

SECTION 7: FAIR COMPETITION
----------------------------------------

7.1 ANTI-TRUST COMPLIANCE
- Price fixing: Not discussed with competitors
- Market allocation: No agreements with competitors
- Bid rigging: Strictly prohibited
- Monopolistic practices: Avoid
- Legal advice: Consult on competition matters

7.2 FAIR BUSINESS PRACTICES
- Accurate advertising: No misleading claims
- Quality standards: Meet specifications
- Delivery commitments: Honor timelines
- Pricing: Transparent and consistent
- Terms: Clear and fair

SECTION 8: INSIDER TRADING
----------------------------------------

8.1 PROHIBITED ACTIVITIES
- Trading on material non-public information
- Tipping: Sharing inside information
- Hedging: Restricted stock transactions
- Short selling: Company stock prohibited
- Blackout periods: Quarter-end to earnings release

8.2 COMPLIANCE PROCEDURES
- Pre-clearance: Required for executives
- Trading windows: Open 3 weeks after earnings
- Holds: 6 months for restricted stock
- Reporting: Monthly statement review
- Training: Annual certification

SECTION 9: ANTI-CORRUPTION
----------------------------------------

9.1 FOREIGN CORRUPT PRACTICES
- Bribes: Prohibited in any form
- Facilitation payments: Not permitted
- Government officials: Special restrictions
- Political contributions: Limited and approved
- Charitable donations: Due diligence required

9.2 RECORD KEEPING
- Accurate books: No off-book accounts
- Approval: All payments authorized
- Documentation: Supporting records required
- Audits: Regular internal reviews
- Transparency: All transactions recorded

SECTION 10: REPORTING VIOLATIONS
----------------------------------------

10.1 REPORTING MECHANISMS
- Manager: First point of contact
- HR: Confidential reporting
- Ethics Hotline: Anonymous, 24/7
- Audit Committee: Serious matters
- Ombudsman: Independent resource

10.2 NON-RETALIATION
- Good faith reports protected
- Retaliation prohibited
- Anonymous reporting available
- Investigation confidentiality
- Disciplinary action for retaliation

SECTION 11: CONSEQUENCES OF VIOLATION
----------------------------------------

11.1 DISCIPLINARY ACTIONS
- Verbal warning: Minor first offense
- Written warning: Repeated minor offenses
- Suspension: Serious violations
- Demotion: Loss of responsibilities
- Termination: Major violations

11.2 LEGAL CONSEQUENCES
- Civil liability: Damages
- Criminal charges: Where applicable
- Regulatory fines: Personal liability
- Reputation damage: Industry impact
- Employment references: Factual only

SECTION 12: ACKNOWLEDGMENT
----------------------------------------

12.1 ANNUAL CERTIFICATION
- All employees must certify annually
- Acknowledgment of policy reading
- Disclosure of any violations
- Training completion confirmation
- Updated contact information

12.2 WAIVERS
- Exceptions require CEO approval
- Board approval for executive waivers
- Temporary vs. permanent waivers
- Disclosed to stakeholders
- Reviewed by legal counsel
