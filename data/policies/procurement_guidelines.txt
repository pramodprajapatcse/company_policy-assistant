==========================================
PROCUREMENT POLICY & GUIDELINES
Version: 5.0 | Effective Date: January 1, 2024
==========================================

SECTION 1: PROCUREMENT PRINCIPLES
----------------------------------------

1.1 CORE PRINCIPLES
- Transparency: All procurements must be documented
- Fair Competition: Minimum 3 vendor quotes required
- Cost Optimization: Best value, not lowest price
- Compliance: Adherence to legal and regulatory requirements
- Sustainability: Preference to environmentally friendly options

1.2 PROCUREMENT HIERARCHY
- Direct Purchase: Up to ₹10,000
- Limited Tender: ₹10,001 - ₹50,000
- Open Tender: ₹50,001 - ₹5,00,000
- Global Tender: Above ₹5,00,000
- Strategic Sourcing: Long-term contracts >1 year

SECTION 2: PURCHASE AUTHORIZATION
----------------------------------------

2.1 FINANCIAL AUTHORITY MATRIX
- Up to ₹10,000: Self-approval (with budget availability)
- ₹10,001 - ₹50,000: Department Head approval
- ₹50,001 - ₹2,00,000: BU Head approval
- ₹2,00,001 - ₹10,00,000: CFO approval
- Above ₹10,00,000: CEO approval + Board ratification

2.2 PURCHASE REQUEST PROCESS
- Step 1: Create PR in ERP system with specifications
- Step 2: Budget verification by Finance
- Step 3: Technical evaluation by user department
- Step 4: Commercial evaluation by Procurement
- Step 5: Approval as per authorization matrix
- Timeline: 5-15 working days based on value

SECTION 3: VENDOR MANAGEMENT
----------------------------------------

3.1 VENDOR REGISTRATION
- Mandatory: All vendors must be registered
- Documentation: GST, PAN, MSME certificate
- Evaluation: Technical, financial, compliance check
- Registration validity: 3 years with annual review
- Blacklisting: For quality issues or ethical violations

3.2 VENDOR EVALUATION CRITERIA
- Quality: 30% weightage
- Price: 30% weightage
- Delivery: 20% weightage
- Service: 10% weightage
- Compliance: 10% weightage
- Minimum qualifying score: 70%

SECTION 4: TENDERING PROCESS
----------------------------------------

4.1 REQUEST FOR PROPOSAL (RFP)
- Scope definition: Clear specifications and deliverables
- Timeline: 15-30 days for vendor responses
- Technical bid: Opened first by technical committee
- Commercial bid: Opened after technical approval
- Negotiation: Allowed only on commercial terms

4.2 EVALUATION COMMITTEE
- Minimum 3 members from different departments
- Independent evaluator for high-value contracts
- Conflict of interest declaration required
- Scoring: Weighted average method
- Recommendation: Detailed evaluation report

SECTION 5: CONTRACT MANAGEMENT
----------------------------------------

5.1 CONTRACT TYPES
- Fixed Price: Clear deliverables, no price variation
- Time & Material: Hourly/daily rates for services
- Rate Contract: Pre-negotiated rates for repeat purchases
- Turnkey: Complete solution including installation
- Framework Agreement: Long-term, call-off basis

5.2 CONTRACT TERMS
- Duration: Typically 1-3 years
- Renewal: Automatic unless terminated with 90 days notice
- Termination: For cause (immediate) or convenience (30 days)
- Penalties: Up to 10% for delayed delivery
- Warranty: Minimum 12 months for equipment

SECTION 6: SPECIAL PROCUREMENT CATEGORIES
----------------------------------------

6.1 IT HARDWARE & SOFTWARE
- Standardization: Approved vendor list only
- Software licenses: Enterprise agreements preferred
- Data security: ISO 27001 certified vendors
- Cloud services: Restricted to approved providers
- Maintenance: AMC mandatory for all hardware

6.2 CAPITAL EQUIPMENT
- ROI analysis: Mandatory for >₹5,00,000
- Lifecycle cost: Include maintenance for 5 years
- Installation: Vendor must provide training
- Spare parts: Availability commitment for 10 years
- Warranty extension: Optional with cost-benefit analysis

6.3 SERVICES
- Statement of Work: Detailed scope mandatory
- Key Personnel: Named resources with CVs
- Service Levels: SLAs with penalties
- Confidentiality: NDA mandatory
- IP Rights: Company retains all intellectual property

SECTION 7: ETHICS & COMPLIANCE
----------------------------------------

7.1 CODE OF CONDUCT
- No acceptance of gifts >₹2,500 from vendors
- Mandatory disclosure of conflicts of interest
- Whistleblower policy for violations
- Anti-bribery compliance
- Annual ethics training for all procurement staff

7.2 DOCUMENT RETENTION
- Purchase orders: 7 years
- Contracts: 10 years after expiry
- Tender documents: 5 years
- Vendor evaluations: 3 years
- Audit trails: Mandatory in ERP system
