==========================================
SAFETY COMPLIANCE MANUAL
Version: 6.0 | Effective Date: January 1, 2024
==========================================

SECTION 1: GENERAL SAFETY POLICY
----------------------------------------

1.1 SAFETY COMMITMENT
- Zero harm workplace objective
- Safety first over production targets
- Regular safety training mandatory
- Near-miss reporting encouraged
- Annual safety audit by external agency

1.2 RESPONSIBILITIES
- Management: Provide safe work environment
- Supervisors: Enforce safety rules
- Employees: Follow all safety procedures
- Contractors: Comply with site safety rules
- Safety Committee: Monthly review meetings

SECTION 2: EMERGENCY PROCEDURES
----------------------------------------

2.1 FIRE SAFETY
- Fire extinguishers: Every 50 sq m area
- Monthly inspection: All fire equipment
- Fire drills: Quarterly for all employees
- Assembly points: Marked with signage
- Emergency exits: Clearly marked and unobstructed

2.2 EVACUATION PROCEDURE
- Alarm activation: Continuous siren
- Evacuation: Immediate, no elevators
- Assembly: Nearest designated point
- Roll call: Department heads responsible
- All-clear: Announced by safety officer

2.3 MEDICAL EMERGENCIES
- First aid: Available in all departments
- Emergency numbers: Posted on notice boards
- Ambulance: On-site for manufacturing
- CPR trained: Minimum 5% employees
- Hospital tie-ups: Within 5 km radius

SECTION 3: PERSONAL PROTECTIVE EQUIPMENT
----------------------------------------

3.1 MANDATORY PPE
- Head protection: Hard hats in factory areas
- Eye protection: Safety glasses for all production
- Footwear: Steel-toed shoes in warehouses
- Hand protection: Cut-resistant gloves
- Hearing protection: Ear plugs above 85 dB

3.2 SPECIAL PPE
- Fall protection: Harnesses for heights >2m
- Respiratory masks: For dust/chemical areas
- Welding shield: For welding operations
- Chemical suits: For hazardous material handling
- High visibility: Vest in warehouse/parking

SECTION 4: WORKPLACE SAFETY
----------------------------------------

4.1 HOUSEKEEPING
- Aisles: Clear and marked
- Spills: Clean immediately
- Storage: Maximum height 2.5m
- Waste: Segregated and disposed daily
- Inspections: Weekly department audits

4.2 MACHINE SAFETY
- Guarding: All moving parts covered
- Lock-out/tag-out: Mandatory for maintenance
- Emergency stops: Tested monthly
- Operator training: Certified before use
- Safety interlocks: Never bypassed

SECTION 5: CHEMICAL SAFETY
----------------------------------------

5.1 HAZARDOUS MATERIALS
- SDS: Available for all chemicals
- Labeling: All containers properly labeled
- Storage: Compatible materials stored together
- Secondary containment: For liquid chemicals
- Spill kit: Available in chemical areas

5.2 CHEMICAL HANDLING
- Training: Before handling any chemicals
- PPE: As specified in SDS
- Ventilation: Local exhaust where required
- Disposal: Authorized hazardous waste vendor
- Inventory: Monthly reconciliation

SECTION 6: ELECTRICAL SAFETY
----------------------------------------

6.1 ELECTRICAL INSTALLATIONS
- Panels: Locked, accessible only to qualified
- Cables: Properly insulated and routed
- GFCI: All outdoor and wet locations
- Extension cords: Temporary use only
- Load calculation: Not exceeding 80% capacity

6.2 WORKING WITH ELECTRICITY
- De-energized work: Preferred method
- Live work: Permit required
- Voltage testing: Before touching
- Arc flash: PPE for high voltage
- Contractor compliance: Audited monthly

SECTION 7: ERGONOMICS
----------------------------------------

7.1 OFFICE ERGONOMICS
- Workstation assessment: Upon request
- Monitor height: Eye level recommended
- Chair: Adjustable with lumbar support
- Breaks: 5 minutes every hour
- Eye strain: 20-20-20 rule (every 20 mins, look 20 feet, 20 seconds)

7.2 MANUAL HANDLING
- Weight limit: 15 kg per person
- Team lifting: For >15 kg items
- Mechanical aids: Trolleys, forklifts available
- Training: Proper lifting techniques
- Reporting: All musculoskeletal injuries

SECTION 8: INCIDENT REPORTING
----------------------------------------

8.1 INCIDENT CLASSIFICATION
- Near miss: No injury but potential
- First aid: Minor treatment
- Medical treatment: Doctor visit required
- Lost time: >24 hours absence
- Fatality: Immediate escalation

8.2 REPORTING PROCEDURE
- Immediate: Notify supervisor
- Investigation: Within 24 hours
- Root cause analysis: For serious incidents
- Corrective actions: Implemented within 7 days
- Trend analysis: Monthly safety meetings

SECTION 9: SAFETY TRAINING
----------------------------------------

9.1 MANDATORY TRAINING
- New hire orientation: Day 1 safety briefing
- Annual refresher: All safety topics
- Fire safety: Every 6 months
- First aid/CPR: Every 2 years
- Specific hazards: Job-specific training

9.2 TRAINING RECORDS
- Database: All employees tracked
- Certification: Valid for specified period
- Refresher: Before certificate expiry
- Contractors: Verification of training
- Audits: Training effectiveness evaluated

SECTION 10: SAFETY AUDITS
----------------------------------------

10.1 INTERNAL AUDITS
- Frequency: Monthly department audits
- Scope: All safety aspects
- Team: Cross-functional safety committee
- Findings: Corrective action plan within 7 days
- Closure verification: Next month's audit

10.2 EXTERNAL AUDITS
- Annual: Third-party safety audit
- Regulatory: As required by law
- Insurance: Bi-annual risk assessment
- Client: Customer-specific requirements
- Certification: ISO 45001 recertification
